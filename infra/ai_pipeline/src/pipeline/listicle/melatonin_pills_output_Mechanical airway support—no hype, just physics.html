1) The real bottleneck is mechanical: your nasal valve

When intensity rises, many athletes feel their nose “pinch” or “collapse.” That’s the nasal valve—the narrowest part of your airway—folding under negative pressure.

Simple explainer: negative pressure + narrow valve = collapse.

If you prefer nasal breathing for control and calm, that collapse is what forces an early switch to mouth breathing. Pharmacy strips often lift at the edges as sweat builds, so they fail exactly when breathing gets hard.

Hypowered’s sport-built strip uses an external spring to gently lift the sidewalls of the nose and support the nasal valve. This increases the valve area and can reduce resistance to airflow—an uncomplicated, mechanical effect.
